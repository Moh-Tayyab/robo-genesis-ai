# Lab: Research Humanoid Robots

## Overview

In this lab, you'll apply what you've learned about humanoid robotics by conducting independent research on robot platforms. You'll practice evaluating robots critically, comparing design trade-offs, and developing informed opinions about the state of the field.

**Duration**: 60-90 minutes

**Prerequisites**: Completion of Lessons 1-3 in this chapter

## Learning Objectives

By completing this lab, you will:
- Develop skills in researching and evaluating robotic platforms
- Practice distinguishing between marketing claims and technical reality
- Learn to assess trade-offs in robot design
- Build a framework for comparing humanoid platforms

## Lab Exercise 1: Deep Dive on a Humanoid Platform

### Task

Choose ONE humanoid robot platform that was NOT covered in detail in the lessons (not Atlas, Figure 01, Optimus, or Unitree G1). Research it thoroughly and create a comprehensive profile.

### Suggested Platforms

- Agility Robotics Digit
- Sanctuary AI Phoenix
- Apptronik Apollo
- 1X Technologies NEO
- Fourier Intelligence GR-1
- UBTECH Walker X
- PAL Robotics TALOS
- SoftBank Robotics NAO or Pepper

### Research Questions

For your chosen platform, answer:

#### 1. Basic Specifications
- Height, weight, degrees of freedom
- Actuation type (hydraulic, electric, hybrid)
- Sensor suite (cameras, LiDAR, IMUs, etc.)
- Estimated runtime on battery
- Price (if publicly available) or estimated cost tier

#### 2. Design Philosophy
- What problem is this robot designed to solve?
- What applications is it optimized for?
- What trade-offs did the designers make? (e.g., speed vs. battery life, complexity vs. robustness)

#### 3. Technical Capabilities
- What can this robot do well?
- What are its demonstrated capabilities? (link to videos if possible)
- What limitations does it have?

#### 4. Unique Features
- What makes this platform different from others?
- Does it have any innovative technologies or approaches?
- Are there any design choices you find particularly interesting?

#### 5. Current Status
- Is this a research prototype, commercial product, or somewhere in between?
- Who can access or purchase it?
- What stage of development is it in?

### Deliverable

Create a 1-2 page document or slide deck presenting your findings. Include:
- Specification table
- Key images (credited appropriately)
- Analysis of design trade-offs
- Your assessment of its strengths and limitations
- Comparison to one of the four platforms from Lesson 2

## Lab Exercise 2: Application-Specific Platform Recommendation

### Scenario

You've been hired as a consultant to recommend a humanoid robot platform for one of the following organizations:

**Scenario A - Hospital Environment**:
- Need: Robot to transport meals, medications, and supplies throughout a hospital
- Environment: Smooth floors, elevators, automatic doors, but also crowded hallways
- Constraints: Must operate safely near patients, medical staff, and visitors
- Budget: $50,000-$150,000 per unit

**Scenario B - Warehouse Logistics**:
- Need: Robot to pick items from shelves and place in bins for shipping
- Environment: Structured warehouse with standardized shelving
- Constraints: Must work 8-hour shifts, handle packages up to 20 kg
- Budget: $30,000-$100,000 per unit, planning to deploy 10 units

**Scenario C - Assisted Living Facility**:
- Need: Robot to help elderly residents with daily tasks (fetching items, reminders, light cleaning)
- Environment: Residential setting with furniture, carpets, stairs
- Constraints: Must be safe and non-threatening, simple for residents to interact with
- Budget: $20,000-$60,000 per unit

### Task

1. **Choose one scenario** to focus on
2. **Analyze requirements** specific to that scenario:
   - What capabilities are essential?
   - What capabilities are nice-to-have?
   - What safety constraints must be met?
   - What reliability level is required?

3. **Recommend a platform** from those you've studied (including Exercise 1)
   - Justify your choice based on technical capabilities
   - Address any gaps between robot capabilities and requirements
   - Discuss risks or limitations
   - Propose mitigations for those limitations

4. **Alternative consideration**: What would you recommend if your first-choice platform wasn't available or proved unsuitable?

### Deliverable

Write a 2-3 page recommendation memo that includes:
- Executive summary (your recommendation in 3-4 sentences)
- Requirements analysis
- Platform comparison (comparing 2-3 options)
- Final recommendation with justification
- Risk assessment and mitigation strategies

## Lab Exercise 3: Future Capabilities Speculation

### Task

Based on your understanding of current challenges and emerging technologies, make informed predictions about humanoid robot capabilities in 5, 10, and 20 years.

### Framework

For each time horizon, predict:

#### 5 Years (2029)
Focus on **incremental improvements** to existing capabilities:
- What capabilities will improve quantitatively? (e.g., runtime, walking speed)
- What demonstrations will become reliable deployments?
- What new applications will emerge?

#### 10 Years (2034)
Consider **significant advances** requiring breakthroughs:
- What current "research demos" will be ready for commercial use?
- What new capabilities might emerge?
- How might manufacturing and cost change?

#### 20 Years (2044)
Think about **transformative change**:
- What might become possible that seems far-fetched today?
- How might the field look fundamentally different?
- What societal changes might widespread humanoid robots enable?

### Grounding Your Predictions

For each prediction, identify:
- What technological advance would enable it
- What current research is moving in that direction
- What obstacles must be overcome
- How confident you are (high/medium/low)

### Deliverable

Create a "Future of Humanoid Robotics" timeline with:
- 3-5 predictions for each time horizon
- Brief justification for each prediction
- Identification of enabling technologies
- Confidence levels

## Try With AI

Now that you've conducted research and made predictions, let's use AI to challenge and refine your thinking.

### Initial Request

> "I've been researching humanoid robots and their applications. I concluded that [your chosen platform from Exercise 2] is best suited for [your chosen scenario] because [brief justification]. Can you play devil's advocate and argue why a different platform or approach might be better? Consider technical limitations, cost-effectiveness, and practical deployment challenges."

### Critical Evaluation

Review the AI's counter-arguments:

- [ ] **Are the criticisms technically valid?** (Based on your research and understanding)
- [ ] **Did the AI identify limitations you overlooked?** (Good challenge to your reasoning)
- [ ] **Are the alternative suggestions realistic?** (Or wishful thinking about robot capabilities?)
- [ ] **Does it consider practical constraints** (cost, availability, maintenance)?

### Focused Update

Respond to the AI's strongest critique:

> "You raised a good point about [specific limitation]. Given that constraint, what modifications or supporting systems would be needed to make my recommended platform viable for this application? Be specific about hardware additions, software requirements, or operational procedures."

### Second Iteration

Test your future predictions:

> "I predicted that by [year], humanoid robots will be able to [specific capability]. Based on current technological trends, is this timeline realistic, too optimistic, or too conservative? What factors might accelerate or delay this capability?"

### Reflection

1. **Challenging Assumptions**: Did the AI's devil's advocate argument reveal assumptions you made without sufficient evidence? How did defending your position strengthen or change your reasoning?

2. **Solution Thinking**: When the AI suggested modifications to address limitations, were they practical engineering solutions or vague aspirations? How can you tell the difference?

3. **Prediction Quality**: When evaluating your timeline predictions, did the AI provide insight into technological dependencies you hadn't considered? Or did it merely agree/disagree without substantive reasoning?

**Key Insight**: Using AI as a critical thinking partner works best when you have enough domain knowledge to:
- Recognize valid criticisms from weak objections
- Evaluate whether proposed solutions are technically feasible
- Assess whether the AI's reasoning is grounded in physics and engineering reality

## Evaluation Criteria

Your work will be assessed on:

- **Technical accuracy**: Specifications and capabilities are correctly represented
- **Critical thinking**: Analysis goes beyond surface-level observations
- **Justification quality**: Claims are supported with evidence and reasoning
- **Trade-off awareness**: Recognizes that design choices involve compromises
- **Practical grounding**: Recommendations are realistic given current technology
- **Clear communication**: Ideas are expressed clearly and organized logically
